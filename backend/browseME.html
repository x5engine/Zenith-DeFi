A Deep Dive into Trustless Cross-Chain Interoperability

The Paradigm Shift

1inch Fusion+ moves beyond traditional, imperative DEX swaps to a declarative, intent-based model. This fundamental change transfers execution risk from the user to professional resolvers, enabling gasless, MEV-protected transactions.

Traditional DEX Swaps (Imperative)

  • User's Burden: User constructs, signs, and broadcasts the transaction.
  • Gas Fees: User pays all network fees directly.
  • MEV Exposure: Vulnerable to front-running and sandwich attacks.
  • Execution Risk: User manages slippage and bears the risk of failed transactions.

1inch Fusion+ Swaps (Intent-Based)

  • User's Burden: User signs a single, off-chain message declaring their intent.
  • Gas Fees: "Gasless" experience; resolvers pay fees, priced into the rate.
  • MEV Exposure: Inherently protected through a private relayer network.
  • Execution Risk: Transferred entirely to the professional resolver.

The Trustless Core: HTLCs

Atomic swaps are powered by Hashed Timelock Contracts (HTLCs), which ensure that a cross-chain trade is "all-or-nothing," eliminating counterparty risk without a centralized bridge.

The Relayer

A centralized 1inch service that broadcasts intents to resolvers and coordinates the swap process.

Resolver Economic Stake

To ensure network integrity, resolvers must stake 1INCH tokens equivalent to at least 5% of the total "Unicorn Power," aligning their incentives with the health of the protocol.

The Four-Phase Swap Lifecycle

1. Announcement

Maker signs intent; Relayer starts Dutch auction.

2. Deposit

Resolver locks funds in HTLC escrows on both chains.

3. Withdrawal

Secret is revealed; both parties withdraw their assets.

4. Recovery

(Optional) Timelock expires; funds are returned.
